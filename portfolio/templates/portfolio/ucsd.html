Master of Sciences in Business Analytics

The Master of Science in Business Analytics (MSBA) at UCSD Rady School of Management is a cutting-edge program designed for the digitally competitive business landscape. It features a full-time, STEM-designated curriculum enhanced with AI and Large Language Models, covering all facets of business analytics. This program, which can be completed in just 11 months, prepares graduates to be at the forefront of business data science, ready for a market projected to grow 25% in the next five years. It's an ideal pathway for those aspiring to integrate AI with business strategy and data-driven decision-making.

Summer Quarter

Special Topics Math and Programming for Business Analytics

This course covers essential mathematics and programming skills for business analytics. It focuses on statistical analysis, linear algebra, and optimization, combined with practical programming in languages like Python or R. Students learn to apply these skills to real-world business scenarios, preparing them for data-driven decision-making in a business context.

Units: 4

Professors: Vincent R. Nijs and Terrence August

Special Topics Big Data Tech SQL and ETL

This course offers an introduction to Big Data technologies, focusing on SQL for database management and the ETL process. Students learn to handle large datasets by mastering SQL queries and developing skills in the ETL process, essential for data preparation and analysis in Big Data environments. The course is tailored for those interested in data management and analytics.

Units: 2

Professors: Johan Perols

Business Analytics in Marketing, Finance, and Operations

Business analytics projects should strive to create substantial value to an organization by solving impactful business problems. In this class, students will learn to identify business opportunities in the substantive areas of marketing, finance, and operations.

Units: 4

Professors: Terrence August
